## Key Research Insights

### From Kuperberg Double-Lattice Theory
The Kuperberg & Kuperberg construction finds optimal packing by:
1. Finding the optimal inscribed parallelogram in the polygon
2. Using its side vectors to generate two interlaced lattices
3. One lattice has copies of K, the other has copies of -K (180° rotated)

**HOWEVER**: This is for CONVEX polygons. Our tree is NOT convex!

### From Kaggle Discussions
- Top teams use "dense 2-tree lattices with alternating up/down trees"
- "180° rotational symmetry patterns" are key
- "Strip-packing algorithms" (Sparrow method) achieve sub-69 scores
- N=2, 4, 8, 14 have "proven optimal packings" (but details not shared)

### From Fractional Translation Kernel
The jonathanchan kernel uses:
1. Ensemble of best-per-N from multiple sources
2. SA with translations
3. **Fractional translation** - tiny (0.001 to 0.00001) step movements in 8 directions
4. Multi-scale optimization

## Recommended Approaches (Priority Order)

### 1. **[HIGHEST PRIORITY] Implement Fractional Translation Technique**
The fractional_translation function from jonathanchan kernel:
