They used `differential_evolution` to find optimal lattice parameters (density ~0.87) for a "Blue/Pink" (up/down) unit cell. They then generated a lattice packing using `lattice_generator_v2.py` and fed it into the C++ solver (`tree_packer_v21`). The experiment achieved a score of 77.66, a significant improvement over the previous 107.76, but still short of the target (68.92).

## Strategic Assessment
[Is this the right thing to be doing?]

**Approach Fit**: YES. Lattice initialization is the only way to beat the target.
**Effort Allocation**: The effort on optimizing the unit cell was well spent.
**Assumptions**: The current generator assumes that a **circular cluster** of lattice points is the best starting point. **THIS IS WRONG.**
**Blind Spots**: **The "Square Peg in Round Hole" Problem.**
- The `lattice_generator_v2.py` sorts candidates by `x^2 + y^2`. This creates a **circular** crop of the lattice.
- The target bounding box is a **square**.
- Fitting a circle into a square wastes $(1 - \pi/4) \approx 21.5\%$ of the area.
- Theoretical Score with Circular Crop: $Score \approx \frac{200 \times 0.2475}{0.87 \times 0.785} \approx 72.5$. (Actual 77.66 is close, slightly worse due to discrete effects).
- Theoretical Score with Square Crop: $Score \approx \frac{200 \times 0.2475}{0.87} \approx 56.9$.
- **Target is 68.92.** A square crop should easily beat it.

**Trajectory**: We are on the brink of success. The lattice is dense enough. We just need to cut it into the right shape.

## What's Working
- **Lattice Density**: The found parameters (density 0.87) are excellent. Do not change them yet.
- **Solver**: The `tree_packer_v21` is effectively refining the local packing.
- **Pipeline**: The generate -> pack -> bp loop is solid.

## Key Concerns
- **Observation**: `lattice_generator_v2.py` uses `candidates.sort(key=lambda p: p['x']**2 + p['y']**2)`.
- **Why it matters**: This creates a circular initial packing. When placed in a square box, the corners are empty. The solver struggles to move trees from the circle edge to the corners (large global moves).
- **Suggestion**: Change the sorting metric to **Chebyshev distance** (L-infinity norm) to create a **Square Crop**.

- **Observation**: `tree_packer_v21` input parsing.
- **Why it matters**: The generator writes `s` prefixes (e.g., `s1.23`). Standard C++ `atof` returns 0.0 for this. The score (77.66) implies it *is* working (otherwise score would be ~217), so the solver likely handles it.
- **Suggestion**: Verify `tree_packer_v21.cpp` handles `s` prefix. If not, the current result is a "miracle" of the solver untangling a pile at (0,0), and fixing it will boost performance even more.
